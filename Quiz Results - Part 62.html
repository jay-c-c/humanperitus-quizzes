If in a given Binomial distribution with ‘n’ fixed and P < 0.5, then (UGC NET Management Jun 2014)

  • B.The Binomial distribution will be skewed to the left

Correct Answer: The Binomial distribution will be skewed to the right

If in a given Binomial distribution with ‘n’ fixed and P < 0.5, then the Binomial distribution will be skewed to the right.

 

The Binomial distribution is a discrete probability distribution that describes the number of successes in a fixed number of independent trials, each with the same probability of success (denoted as p). The distribution is skewed to the right when p < 0.5, meaning that the distribution is pulled towards the left and has a longer tail on the right-hand side.

 

As p approaches 0, the Binomial distribution becomes increasingly skewed to the right and can be approximated by the Poisson distribution. However, the Poisson distribution is a limiting case of the Binomial distribution and is not always a good approximation for small values of n or large values of p.

While the guideline of considering a sample size less than 30 as small is commonly used, it's important to note that this is not an absolute rule.
