Specifically, it is a measure of how much energy the emissions of 1 ton of a gas will absorb over a given period of time, relative to the emissions of 1 ton of carbon dioxide (CO2). Question 26
The relation ‘A is father of B’ is:
  • A.non-symmetrical
  • B.symmetrical
  • C.asymmetrical
  • D.transitive
Correct Answer: asymmetrical

Transitivity of a relation means that if one element is related to another, and that second element is related to a third, then the first element is related to the third as well. For example, if A is taller than B, and B is taller than C, then A must be taller than C. An intransitive relation is the opposite: if A is related to B, and B is related to C, then A cannot be related to C. For example, if A is next in line to B, and B is next in line to C, then A cannot be next in line to C.

 

Symmetry of a relation means that if A is related to B, then B is related to A. For example, if A is a cousin of B, then B is a cousin of A. Asymmetry is the opposite: if A is related to B, then B cannot be related to A. For example, if A is the father of B, then B cannot be the father of A.

 

Reflexivity of a relation means that every element is related to itself. For example, being the same height as is reflexive because everyone is the same height as themselves. Irreflexivity is the opposite: no element is related to itself. For example, being taller than is irreflexive because no one is taller than themselves.

 

An equivalence relation is one that is transitive, symmetric, and reflexive. For example, the relation of being identical is an equivalence relation because it satisfies all three properties: if A is identical to B and B is identical to C, then A is identical to C; if A is identical to B, then B is identical to A; and every element is identical to itself.

Fallacy of Composition occurs when someone mistakenly assumes that what is true for the individual parts of a whole must also be true for the whole collectively. In this case, the argument assumes that because all humans are mortal, the human race as a whole must also come to an end.

 

Here's a breakdown of the argument:

 

Premise: "All humans are mortal."

Conclusion: "Therefore, the human race must someday come to an end."

 

The fallacy lies in assuming that the characteristic of mortality, which applies to individual humans, automatically applies to the human race as a collective entity. While it is true that individual humans have a finite lifespan and eventually die, it does not necessarily follow that the entire human race will cease to exist.

 

The fallacy of composition arises from the mistaken belief that properties or characteristics of the parts of a whole necessarily apply to the whole. In this case, the argument incorrectly generalizes the mortality of individual humans to the entire human race.

 

To avoid the fallacy of composition, it is important to recognize that the properties or characteristics of individual parts do not always extend to the whole. The behavior or attributes of individual elements may not reflect the behavior or attributes of the collective group as a whole. Deductive arguments aim to provide conclusive proof of their conclusions. If the premises are true and the argument is valid (meaning the conclusion logically follows from the premises), then the argument is considered sound. If the argument is not valid, it is considered invalid, regardless of the truth of the premises.

 

Inductive arguments, on the other hand, aim to support their conclusions with evidence, but they do not guarantee the truth of the conclusion. Instead, they suggest that the conclusion is likely based on the premises. A cogent inductive argument is one that is strong and has true premises, while an uncogent inductive argument is either weak or has false premises.

Question 10
Given below are two statements: (UGC NET 24thMar 2023 Evening Shift)Statement I: Article 48A: It is the duty of citizens of India to protect and improve the natural environment.Statement II: Article 51: Directive Principles of state policy are to ensure protection and improvement of the natural Environment.In light of the above statements, choose the correct answer from the options given below:
  • A.Both Statement I and Statement II are true.
  • B.Both Statement I and Statement II are false.
  • C.Statement I is true but Statement II is false.
  • D.Statement I is false but Statement II is true.
Correct Answer: Both Statement I and Statement II are false.

Statement I is false. While Article 48A of the Indian Constitution does indeed mention environmental protection, it states that it is the duty of the State to protect and improve the environment, not the individual citizens.

 

Statement II is also false. Article 51 of the Indian Constitution is a directive principle related to international peace and security, and it does not specifically address environmental protection.
