Question 3
The sequence of stages of active listening is: (UGC NET 5thJan 2022 Morning paper)A. UnderstandingB. RememberingC. RespondingD. ReceivingE. EvaluatingChoose the correct answer from the options given below:
  • A.A, B, C, E, D
  • B.C, A, D, B, E
  • C.D, A, E, B, C
  • D.B, C, E, D, A
Correct Answer: D, A, E, B, C

The sequence of stages of active listening typically follows this order:

 

1. Receiving: The first stage of active listening is receiving. It involves being physically present and paying attention to the speaker. Receiving includes using your senses to perceive the verbal and non-verbal cues of the speaker, such as their words, tone of voice, facial expressions, and body language.

 

2. Understanding: After receiving the message, the listener moves on to the stage of understanding. This stage involves comprehending the message, interpreting its meaning, and grasping the speaker's intended message. It requires actively processing and making sense of the information being conveyed.

 

3. Evaluating: The next stage is evaluating. At this stage, the listener critically assesses the information, analyzes its validity, considers alternative perspectives, and forms opinions or judgments based on the message and its content. Evaluating allows the listener to think critically and engage in a deeper analysis of the information.

 

4. Remembering: Remembering is the stage where the listener retains the information and stores it in memory for future reference. It involves recalling and retaining key details, main ideas, and important points from the speaker's message.

 

5. Responding: The final stage of active listening is responding. This stage involves providing feedback or a response to the speaker. It can include verbal responses, such as asking questions, providing comments, or offering reflections, as well as non-verbal responses, such as nodding or maintaining eye contact. Responding shows engagement, confirms understanding, and encourages further communication.

According to Roland Barthes, the sequence of narrative codes is: (20th Oct 2022 Evening shift)A. ActionB. SymbolicC. SemanticD. EnigmaE. ReferentialChoose the correct answer from the options given below
  • A.B, C, D, E, A
  • B.C, D, A, E, B
  • C.D, E, B, A, C
  • D.A, C, D, E, B
Correct Answer: A, C, D, E, B

According to Roland Barthes, the sequence of narrative codes is typically as follows:

 

1. Action: The first code in the sequence is the action code. This refers to the physical and observable events or actions that take place within the narrative. It involves the basic plot elements and the development of the story through actions and events.

 

2. Semantic: The second code is the semantic code. This code focuses on the meaning and significance of the narrative elements. It delves into the deeper layers of the story, examining the symbolism, themes, and broader implications conveyed through the narrative.

 

3. Enigma: The third code is the enigma code. This code introduces a sense of mystery, suspense, or unresolved questions within the narrative. It involves the inclusion of puzzles, secrets, or unresolved elements that captivate the audience's attention and keep them engaged in the story.

 

4. Referential: The fourth code is the referential code. This code relates to the references made within the narrative to external elements such as cultural, historical, or social contexts. It includes allusions, references to real-world events, or connections to specific cultural symbols or contexts that enrich the meaning of the narrative.

 

5. Symbolic: The final code in the sequence is the symbolic code. This code involves the use of symbols and symbolic elements within the narrative. Symbols are objects, actions, or characters that represent or stand for something else, often carrying deeper meanings or associations. The symbolic code adds layers of interpretation and invites the audience to explore different levels of understanding within the story.
