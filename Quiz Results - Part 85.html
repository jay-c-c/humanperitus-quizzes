Question 5
Arrange the steps of project method in a sequence. (UGC NET 12thMar 2023 Evening)A. Purpose of the projectB. Providing a situationC. Execution of the projectD. Planning of the projectE. Recording of the projectF. Evaluation of the projectChoose the correct answer from the options given below:
  • A.A, D, B, C, E, F
  • B.D, B, A, E, F, C
  • C.B, A, D, C, F, E
  • D.C, A, D, E, F, B
Correct Answer: B, A, D, C, F, E

The steps of project method in a sequence are as follows.

1. Providing a situation: The project method usually starts with providing a real-world situation or problem that requires investigation, analysis, and solution.

2. Purpose of the project: Once the situation is presented, the purpose and objectives of the project are defined. This step helps clarify the goals of the project and what is expected to be achieved.

3. Planning of the project: In this step, students plan how they will approach the project, including deciding on the scope, resources needed, timelines, and strategies for research and implementation.

4. Execution of the project: After planning, students carry out the project activities. They gather information, conduct research, experiment, create, or perform, depending on the nature of the project.

5. Evaluation of the project: Once the project is completed, an evaluation is conducted. This involves assessing the project's outcomes, the process followed, and the skills and knowledge acquired by the learners.

6. Recording of the project: Throughout the project, students maintain records of their work, observations, data, and reflections. This documentation is important for assessing their progress and outcomes.

  • C.A-II, B-IV, C-I., D-III
  • D.A-I, B-III, C-IV, D-II
Correct Answer: A-II, B-IV, C-I., D-III

The Cognitive Apprenticeship Model is an instructional approach that involves guiding learners through a process of acquiring skills and knowledge by providing structured support and guidance. The model emphasizes learning through observation, practice, and interaction with an expert or more knowledgeable individual.

a. Modelling - Observing the performance of an expert (Teacher or more experienced peer):

Modelling involves an expert demonstrating how to perform a task or solve a problem. Learners observe the expert's actions, processes, and decision-making. This helps learners gain insights into the strategies and thought processes required for successful task completion.

b. Coaching - Giving external support to the learner in the form of hints, feedback, reminders, etc.:

Coaching involves the expert providing ongoing support and guidance to the learner. This support can include giving hints, offering feedback, providing reminders, and helping the learner overcome challenges. The goal is to assist the learner in developing their skills while offering targeted assistance.

c. Scaffolding - Helping the learner in starting and gradually fading the support:

Scaffolding refers to the process of providing temporary support to learners as they engage in tasks that are initially beyond their current capabilities. This support is gradually reduced as learners become more competent. The idea is to give just enough assistance to enable successful performance while encouraging independent learning over time.

d. Articulation - Giving words to their understanding of the process and content:

Articulation involves having learners explain their thought processes, strategies, and understanding of the content. By verbalizing their understanding, learners internalize the knowledge and clarify their own thinking. This process helps them solidify their understanding and make their learning explicit.
