Observational learning, a key concept in Albert Bandura's Social Cognitive Theory, involves individuals learning by observing others. The elements of observation learning are as follows:

Question 27
Arrange the five Phases of "Constructivist Learning Approach" in a Sequence. (UGC NET 06thMar 2023 Evening)A. ExploreB. ElaborateC. EvaluateD. ExplainE. EngageChoose the correct answer from the options given below:

  • B.D, A, B, E, C
  • C.C, D, A, E, B
  • D.E, A, D, B, C
Correct Answer: E, A, D, B, C

The five phases of the Constructivist Learning Approach are:

1. Engage: In this initial phase, learners are introduced to the learning topic or concept. The goal is to capture their interest and activate their prior knowledge. This can be done through activities, discussions, questions, or real-world scenarios that grab the learners' attention and make them curious about the subject.

2. Explore: In this phase, learners delve deeper into the topic by actively exploring and investigating it. They might engage in hands-on activities, research, problem-solving tasks, or discussions. The emphasis is on allowing learners to discover concepts on their own, fostering a sense of ownership over their learning process.

3. Explain: Once learners have had a chance to explore and experiment, the "explain" phase comes into play. During this phase, learners reflect on their experiences and connect them to theoretical concepts. They may share their findings with peers, present their understanding, and clarify any misconceptions.

4. Elaborate: In this phase, learners build upon their foundational understanding and expand their knowledge. They might work on more complex tasks, engage in creative projects, or explore related topics. This phase encourages learners to apply their understanding in novel ways, promoting deeper comprehension and critical thinking.

5. Evaluate: The final phase involves assessing learners' understanding and skills. This assessment can be both formative (ongoing feedback during the learning process) and summative (final evaluation of learning outcomes). The evaluation phase helps both learners and educators understand the extent to which the learning objectives have been achieved and whether any adjustments or additional support are needed.

Ivan Pavlov conducted a renowned experiment in classical conditioning, where he trained dogs to associate the sound of a buzzer with being fed. This led to the development of key concepts:

• Conditioned Response: The learned response to a conditioned stimulus, like salivating in response to a bell or tuning fork.

  • D.Students sit in ‘C’ shapeformation with teacher at the centre.
Correct Answer: Some students sit close together in a circle in the centre of classroom while the rest of students stand at the back of classroom and observe the group discussion.

A fishbowl conversation is a form of dialog that can be used when discussing topics within large groups. Fishbowl conversations are sometimes also used in participatory events such as conferences. The advantage of fishbowl is that it allows the entire group to participate in a conversation. Several people can join the discussion.

 

 

Method of fishbowl conversation:

Four to five chairs are arranged in an inner circle. This is the fishbowl. The remaining chairs are arranged in concentric circles outside the fishbowl. A few participants are selected to fill the fishbowl, while the rest of the group sit on the chairs outside the fishbowl. In an open fishbowl, one chair is left empty. In a closed fishbowl, all chairs are filled. The moderator introduces the topic and the participants start discussing the topic. The audience outside the fishbowl listen in on the discussion.

It aims to provide teachers with opportunities for practice, reflection, and feedback in a controlled environment. The conditions necessary for effective micro teaching include:

 

Controlled environment: Micro teaching takes place in a controlled setting, typically a simulated or real classroom, where the teacher can focus on specific teaching skills or techniques.

Question 29
Programmed instruction consists of a network of statements and tests, which direct the student to new statements depending on his pattern of errors. It is based on a particular tool which is called _______________.
  • A.Teaching Machine
  • B.Patterned Machine
  • C.Response Machine
  • D.Constructed Machine
Correct Answer: Teaching Machine

Programmed instruction is based on a particular tool called the "Teaching Machine." The Teaching Machine is a mechanical or electronic device that presents programmed instructional materials to students. It consists of a network of statements and tests that guide the student's learning process based on their pattern of errors. The machine provides immediate feedback and allows students to progress through the instructional material at their own pace. It was developed as a means to deliver self-paced and individualized instruction efficiently.
