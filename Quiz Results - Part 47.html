Question 14
Given below are two statements regarding storage devices on a computer: (UGC NET 4thDec 2021- Evening shift)Statement I: Access time is typically stated in milliseconds for secondary storage devices and in nanoseconds for primary storage devices.Statement II: Average access time can usually be improved by defragmenting the data contents of a hard disk.In the light of the above statements, choose the correct answer from the options givenbelow:
  • A.Both Statement I and Statement II are true
  • B.(B)Both Statement I and Statement II are false
  • C.(C)Statement I is true but Statement II is false
  • D.(D)Statement I is false but Statement II is true
Correct Answer: Both Statement I and Statement II are true

The access time of storage devices varies depending on whether they are primary or secondary storage. For secondary storage devices like hard disk drives (HDDs), access time is measured in milliseconds due to their mechanical components, which introduce latency as the read/write head moves to the desired location. In contrast, primary storage devices such as RAM (Random Access Memory) offer much faster access times, measured in nanoseconds, because they are electronic and lack moving parts.

 

Defragmentation is a process used to improve the access time of secondary storage devices, like hard drives. Over time, files stored on a hard disk can become fragmented, meaning that parts of a file are scattered across different physical locations on the disk. This fragmentation can lead to increased access times since the read/write head must move to various locations to access all the parts of a file. Defragmentation reorganizes the data in a more contiguous manner, reducing the time required to access data and enhancing the average access time of the hard disk.

 

Therefore, both Statement I and Statement II are true.
