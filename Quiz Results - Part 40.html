  • D.Shanghai ranking
Correct Answer: Madrid ranking

Option C is correct.

 

Times Higher Education World University Rankings provide the definitive list of the world’s best universities, with an emphasis on the research mission. It is the only global university league table to judge research-intensive universities across all of their core missions: teaching (the learning environment); research environment (volume, income and reputation); research quality (the outputs of research); industry (knowledge transfer) and international outlook (staff, students and research). The league table was originally compiled and issued by Shanghai Jiao Tong University in 2003, making it the first global university ranking with multifarious indicators. It is recognized as the precursor of global university rankings and the most trustworthy one. ARWU presents the world's top 1000 research universities annually.

Question 18
Which one of the following is not true? (UGC NET Commerce Nov 2017)
  • A.Material resources are subject to laws of mechanics and they cannot have an output greater than the input.
  • B.Human resources are capable of exponential growth and development.

  • D.In current scenario, gradually, the emphasis has shifted from muscle power to use of mind power.
Correct Answer: Human resources cannot yield an output that may be far greater than what the value of their input warrants.

Material Resources:

Material resources typilcally refer to tangible assets such as raw materials, machinery, and physical infrastructure. These resources are subject to the laws of physics and mechanics, meaning that their output is generally constrained by physical limitations. In other words, the output from material resources cannot exceed the input in a way that violates the laws of conservation of energy or other physical principles. There are efficiency constraints, and generally, material resources do not exhibit exponential growth or development.

 

Human Resources:

Human resources, on the other hand, refer to the people working within an organization. Unlike material resources, human resources are not subject to the same physical constraints. Human capabilities, including intellectual and creative capacities, can lead to exponential growth and development. Human resources have the potential to generate outputs that are not strictly bound by the same physical limitations as material resources.

 

(A) Material resources are subject to laws of mechanics and they cannot have an output greater than the input:

True. Material resources are bound by physical laws, and their output is generally constrained by the input, following principles like the conservation of energy.

 

(B) Human resources are capable of exponential growth and development:

True. Human resources, with their intellectual and creative capacities, can experience exponential growth and development, allowing for continuous learning and innovation.

 

(C) Human resources cannot yield an output that may be far greater than what the value of their input warrants:

Not true. Human resources, through creativity and innovation, can generate outputs that surpass the expected value of their input.

 

(D) In the current scenario, gradually, the emphasis has shifted from muscle power to the use of mind power:

True. With technological advancements, there has been a shift from manual labor (muscle power) to knowledge-based work, emphasizing intellectual capabilities (mind power).
