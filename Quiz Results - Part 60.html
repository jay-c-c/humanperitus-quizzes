While using Markov Analysis, which one of the following assumptions is incompatible? (UGC NET Management Nov 2017)
  • A.The system has an infinite set of possible outcomes
  • B.Transition probabilities are constant over a given length of time
  • C.The condition of the system in any given period depends on preceding period condition
  • D.Changes in the system may occur only once during each period
Correct Answer: The system has an infinite set of possible outcomes

Markov analysis is a specialized method tailored for systems characterized by uncertain transitions between different states or conditions over time. It finds practical application in scenarios like assessing the likelihood of a machine's operational status changing from functioning to breakdown within a specific time frame. Similarly, it can be employed to calculate the probability of a customer switching from one cereal brand to another in successive months.

 

This particular application, often referred to as the "brand-switching" problem, serves as a useful illustration to elucidate the fundamental principles of Markov analysis. By studying these transitions and their associated probabilities, we can gain valuable insights into the dynamic behavior of systems, making Markov analysis a valuable tool in fields like reliability engineering, customer behavior analysis, and beyond.

 

First Order Markov process is based on the following three assumptions:

(i) The set of possible outcomes is finite

(ii) The probability of next outcome (state) depends only on the immediately preceding outcome.

(iii) The transition probabilities are constant over time.

A simple Markov Process is discrete and constant over time. A system is said to be discrete in time if it is examined at regular intervals, e.g. daily, monthly or yearly.

In the context of forecasting, there are two broad categories of methods: qualitative and quantitative.

 

Qualitative Forecasting Methods:

Qualitative methods rely on subjective opinions, judgments, and expert insights to make predictions about future events or trends. These methods are used when historical data is insufficient, or when conditions are highly uncertain and rapidly changing. Here are some common qualitative forecasting methods:

1. Executive Opinions: High-level managers, often from various departments like marketing, operations, and finance, come together to collectively develop a forecast based on their experience and expertise.

2. Salesforce Opinions: The sales or customer service staff, due to their direct interaction with customers, can provide valuable insights about customer behavior and potential future trends.

4. Delphi Method: This iterative process aims to achieve a consensus forecast by circulating questionnaires among knowledgeable individuals. It maintains anonymity, encouraging honest responses and minimizing the influence of any single person's opinion.

 

Quantitative Forecasting Methods:

Quantitative methods, in contrast, rely on historical data and mathematical models to make predictions. These methods are used when there is a rich dataset available, and when the future is expected to resemble the past to a certain extent. Here are some common quantitative forecasting methods:

1. Moving Average: A method that calculates a forecast based on a rolling average of the most recent data points. It smooths out short-term fluctuations.

2. Exponential Smoothing: Similar to moving averages, but it gives more weight to recent observations, making it particularly sensitive to recent trends.

3. Time Series Analysis: Involves analyzing historical data to identify patterns, trends, and seasonal variations.

4. Regression Analysis: Utilizes statistical models to understand the relationship between one or more independent variables and the variable being forecasted.

Question 22
Under which method, there is a minimisation of the sum of the squares of deviation between the observed and the fitted values of the data?
  • A.Graphical Method
  • B.Least squares Method
  • C.Exponential Trend Method
  • D.None of the above
Correct Answer: Least squares Method

The least squares method is a mathematical technique used in regression analysis to find the best-fitting line or curve that represents the relationship between two or more variables. It's called "least squares" because it aims to minimize the sum of the squared differences (deviations) between the observed data points and the values predicted by the fitted line or curve.

 

Here's how it works:

1. Data Collection: First, you gather data on the variables of interest. For example, if you're trying to understand the relationship between the number of hours studied and exam scores, you collect data on study hours and corresponding exam scores.

2. Plotting the Data: Next, you plot the data points on a graph, with one variable on the x-axis (independent variable) and the other on the y-axis (dependent variable).

3. Fitting the Line or Curve: The goal is to find a line or curve that best represents the relationship between the variables. This line is determined by adjusting its slope and intercept. For a linear relationship, the line will have the form y = mx + b, where m is the slope and b is the intercept.

4. Calculating Deviations: Once you have the fitted line, you calculate the vertical distance (deviation) between each data point and the corresponding point on the line. This is done for all data points.

5. Squaring Deviations: Each deviation is squared. This is done to ensure that both positive and negative deviations contribute to the total.

6. Summing Squared Deviations: The squared deviations are then summed together.

7. Minimizing the Sum of Squares: The goal is to adjust the line's parameters (slope and intercept) so that the sum of the squared deviations is minimized. This is done using calculus and optimization techniques.

8. Interpreting the Results: Once the line with the minimized sum of squares is determined, it represents the best-fitting relationship between the variables.

 

The least squares method is widely used in various fields, including statistics, economics, engineering, and the physical sciences. It's a powerful tool for analyzing and modeling relationships between variables in real-world data.

Market prices refer to the prices at which the goods and services are sold in the market.

 

National product at factor cost refers to the total value of all final goods and services produced by a country's residents within its borders in a given year, including subsidies but excluding indirect taxes. Factor cost refers to the cost of producing the goods and services, including the cost of labor, capital, and materials.

 

The relationship between national product at market prices and national product at factor cost can be expressed using the following formula:

 

National product at factor cost = National product at market prices - Indirect taxes + Subsidies

By subtracting the indirect taxes and adding the subsidies, we can arrive at the national product at factor cost. National product at factor cost is a more accurate measure of the true value of goods and services produced in a country, as it accounts for the cost of production.
