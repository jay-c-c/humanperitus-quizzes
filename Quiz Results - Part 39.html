  • B.B, C, E only
  • C.A, B, C only
  • D.A, B, C, D only

"To improve access to higher education": One of the primary objectives of Open Distance Learning (ODL) and online programs is to make higher education more accessible to a broader range of individuals, including those who may not have easy access to traditional brick-and-mortar institutions.

 

"To increase Gross Enrolment Ratio (GER)": Increasing the GER is an important goal of ODL and online programs. GER is a measure of the proportion of eligible students in a country who are enrolled in higher education. ODL and online programs can help increase this ratio by providing opportunities to a larger number of students.

 

"To provide opportunities for lifelong learning": ODL and online programs often cater to individuals who wish to pursue education at various stages of their lives, not just during their traditional college years. Lifelong learning is encouraged through these programs, allowing people to acquire new skills and knowledge throughout their lives.

Question 29
Networking of libraries through electronic media is known as:
  • A.Inflibnet
  • B.Libinfnet
  • C.Internet
  • D.HTML
Correct Answer: Inflibnet

Option A is correct.

 

Information and Library Network (INFLIBNET) Centre is an Autonomous Inter-University Centre (IUC) of University Grants Commission, Government of India, involved in creating infrastructure for sharing of library and information resources and services among Academic and Research Institutions through electronic media. INFLIBNET works collaboratively with Indian university libraries to shape the future of the academic libraries in the evolving information environment.  It is a major National Programme initiated by the UGC in 1991 with its Head Quarters at Gujarat University Campus, Ahmedabad.

Question 30
The first virtual university of India came up in
  • A.Andhra Pradesh
  • B.Maharashtra
  • C.Uttar Pradesh
  • D.Tamil Nadu
Correct Answer: Tamil Nadu

Option D is correct.

 

A virtual university provides higher education programs through electronic media, typically the Internet. Some are bricks-and-mortar institutions that provide online learning as part of their extended university courses while others solely offer online courses. They are regarded as a form of distance education. 

 

Seven Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) teamed with Indian Institute of Science (IISc) to set up India's first home-grown virtual technology university. Initially, the online NPTEL content delivered by the IITs was hosted on the website of IIT Madras (Tamil Nadu). The program named as National Programme on Technology Enhanced Learning (NPTEL), was funded by the Ministry of Human Resource Development (MHRD), provides e-learning through online Web and Video courses in Engineering, Sciences, Technology, Management and Humanities.
