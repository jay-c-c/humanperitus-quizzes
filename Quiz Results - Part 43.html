Question 6
Which of the following are the key components of a third-generation balanced scorecard?1. Destination Statement2. Strategic Objectives3. Strategic Linkage Model and Perspective4. Measures and InitiativesChoose the correct answer from the options given below: (UGC NET HRM Jun 2021)
  • A.1 and 2 only
  • B.1, 2 and 3 only
  • C.1, 2, 3 and 4
  • D.3 and 4 only
Correct Answer: 1, 2, 3 and 4

The Balanced Scorecard, devised by Kaplan and Norton, serves as an evaluative tool for assessing Human Resource Management effectiveness. The third-generation iteration of this scorecard emerged in the late 1990s, aimed at rectifying inherent design flaws found in earlier versions. What sets apart this evolved version are its components and the approach used for their development.

 

This updated Balanced Scorecard comprises four principal components:

 

(i) Destination or Vision Statement: Envisioning the organization's future post the successful implementation of the ongoing strategy. This portrayal of the triumphant future is categorized into perspectives such as financial and stakeholder expectations, customer and external relationships, processes and activities, as well as organization and culture.

 

(ii) Strategic Linkage Model: Resembling the traditional strategy map, it typically holds 12-24 strategic objectives segmented into two perspectives – activities and outcomes, similar to the logical framework. These linkages illustrate the proposed cause-and-effect relationships between strategic objectives.

 

(iii) Definitions for Each Strategic Objective: A collection of elucidations outlining the essence of each strategic objective.

 

(iv) Definitions for Chosen Measures: This section entails definitions for measures selected to track each strategic objective, incorporating predetermined targets.

Option B is the correct answer.

 

Theories or Approaches of International Compensation

Different MNCs adopt different approaches for determining compensation. Following are the approaches to international compensation management:

 

1) Localisation Approach: Under Localisation approach, MNC determines compensation on the basis of salary levels for similar jobs in comparable organisations in the host country. This approach is beneficial for those employees moving from developing countries to advance countries, however, may not be encouraged by those moving from advanced to developing countries.

 

2) Negotiation or Bargaining Approach: Under this approach, MNCs determine compensation packages through mutual negotiations between the employee and employer. However, this approach will be successful when the number of expatriates is less and employee and employer both have full knowledge about on-going salary levels and cost of living in the country of placement. This approach may produce dissatisfaction or feel of inequality among host country nationals.

 

3) Lump sum Approach: Under this approach, MNC determine the compensation as a lump sum amount which include basic salary and all kinds of allowances and monetary value of all benefits. Under this approach, employees have freedom to allocate the money for various kinds of expenditure.

 

4) Buffet Approach: Under buffet approach, MNCs provide less cash and more benefits in compensation package. Sometimes expatriates also prefer this method to reduce tax liability and save more so as to repatriate the savings. Employees are given option to decide the cash and benefits component of the compensation package. This approach, however, increases the administration cost of the company as each employee will prefer different choices.

 

5) Cluster Systems Approach: MNCs categorize countries or cities into clusters based on common factors like cost of living or hardship or danger levels. Company determines more or less same compensation for the jobs within same cluster of cities or countries. This approach reduces the administration costs and also enhances the feeling of equality among employees of different countries.

 

6) Global Approach: Some jobs have same requirements with regard to skills, knowledge and competencies throughout the world. Under global approach, MNCs determine uniform pay scales for such jobs throughout all countries of operation. Further, these are payable uniformly for all categories of employees including host country nationals. This approach creates feeling of equality among all expatriates and host country nationals.

 

7) Performance based compensation Approach: Most recently, MNCs are framing compensation packages based on employees’ performance. Under this approach, a minimum base salary is guaranteed to meet basic needs of employees, over and above base salary, employees get the opportunity to earn high salaries as per their performance irrespective of the nationality. This approach helps in increasing the organisation performance and productivity.

 

8) Balance Sheet Approach: Under this approach, MNCs try to balance the home country salary and inconveniences and hardships faced by the expatriates in the host country by providing high salary and other benefits and allowances etc. MNCs offer higher salaries than that of home country and give benefits and allowances like tax equalisation allowance, exchange rate protection allowance, relocation allowance, educational allowance, house rent allowance etc.
