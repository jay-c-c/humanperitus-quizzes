Question 13

  • A.Major premise
  • B.Minor premise
  • C.Conclusion
  • D.Both major and minor premises
Correct Answer: Minor premise

Nyaya syllogism has five and the Aristotelian has three propositions, however, the terms in both are only three- the sadhya or the major, the paksha or the minor and the linga or the middle.

In Nyaya-

The first is called Pratijna-logical statement to be proved. Example-This Hill has fire.

The second is Hetu which states the reason for establishment of the proposition. Example-Because it has smoke.

The third is called Udaharana which gives universal concomitance together with an example.Like-Whatever has smoke has a fire example an oven

The Fourth is Upananya the application of universal concomitance to the present case.Example-The hill has smoke which is invariably associated with fire

The fifth is Nigamana or conclusion drawn from preceding propositions.Example-This hill has fire.

Out of the five Propositions, if we omit the first two then the last three propostions correspond to the major premise, minor premise and the conclusion of the Aristotelian syllogism.Therefore Upanaya correspond to minor premise.

Question 17
Vyaptirelation is the relation between __________ and _______?
  • A.Hetu,Sadhya
  • B.Hetu,Paksa
  • C.Paksa,Sadhya
  • D.Linga,Hetu
Correct Answer: Hetu,Sadhya

It refers to the relation of invariable concomitance or the universal concomitance between the hetu (reason or middle term) and the sadhya (what is to be proved) in inference (anumāna).

In simpler terms, vyapti signifies the relationship of correlation or connection between the reason and the effect, where the presence of the reason (hetu) implies the presence of the effect (sadhya), and the absence of the reason implies the absence of the effect.

For example- smoke and fire:

The hetu – Wherever there is smoke (middle term/hetu).

The sadhya – There is fire (effect/sadhya).

Vyapti asserts that wherever there is smoke (hetu), there is fire (sadhya), establishing a universal connection between the presence of smoke and the presence of fire.
